About LifeLink

We are a technology-first health platform dedicated to bridging the critical gap between blood donors and patients across India. Our goal is to leverage instant updates to save lives in real time.

Our Mission

To establish a reliable, cloud-based, real-time database of voluntary blood donors in India that patients and hospital administrators can search instantaneously in critical times. We aim to ensure that no life is lost due to a delay in sourcing blood.

Our Vision

To cultivate a society where blood donations are entirely voluntary and readily available. Through technology, we aim to streamline the process, optimize donor availability, and reduce emergency wait times to zero.

Eligibility Criteria (India Guidelines)

Before registering as a blood donor on LifeLink, please ensure you satisfy the following standard healthcare eligibility criteria defined by national authorities:

  • Age: You must be between 18 and 65 years old.
  • Weight: You must weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs). Donors below 50kg are not permitted.
  • Hemoglobin level: Minimum 12.5 g/dL.
  • Donation Interval: At least 90 days (3 months) for males and 120 days for females since the last whole blood donation.
  • Health: You must be in good general health, not suffering from acute infections, chronic heart/renal issues, or under active antibiotics.

Benefits of Donating Blood

Donating blood is not just a noble act that saves lives; it also offers physiological benefits to the donor:

  • Stimulates Cell Production: Encourages the body to produce new red blood cells to replenish the supply.
  • Free Health Screening: Every donation includes a mini-physical, checking pulse, blood pressure, temperature, and hemoglobin levels.
  • Reduces Harmful Iron Stores: Helps lower blood viscosity and excess iron levels, supporting cardiovascular health.
  • Psychological Satisfaction: The knowledge that you have directly helped save a life boosts mental well-being.

Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions about blood donation? We have got you covered.

How much blood is taken during donation?

Standard whole blood donations collect exactly 350ml or 450ml of blood depending on the donor's weight and parameters. This is only about 8-10% of the average adult's blood volume, and your body replenishes the fluid volume within 24 hours.

How often can I donate blood in India?

According to Indian National Blood Transfusion Council guidelines, healthy male donors can donate blood once every 3 months (90 days) and female donors once every 4 months (120 days).

Is blood donation painful or unsafe?

It is completely safe. Every needle and kit used is sterile, disposable, and opened in front of you, eliminating any risk of contracting bloodborne infections. You will feel a quick, minor pinch when the needle is inserted, followed by a standard resting period.

How long does the donation process take?

The actual blood extraction takes only 8 to 10 minutes. However, the entire process—including check-in, a brief medical screening, and resting for refreshments afterwards—takes about 1 hour total.
